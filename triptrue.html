OTA vs Direct rule — Expedia and Kayak are great for finding the lowest price but book direct on the airline or hotel website. You get better cancellation rights, no OTA service fees, and the airline will actually help you if something goes wrong. Use OTAs to find, use direct to book.
Geo-pricing is real — the same flight can cost less when searched from a different country's version of the booking site. Before buying, try searching on Google Flights with the destination country selected as your region. VPNs can also unlock lower regional fares on international routes.

Pro Tip: Southwest includes 2 free checked bags. Allegiant/Spirit/Frontier bags often cost more than the base fare.
🗺️ Hidden Route Strategies: Beyond splitting airlines, consider: (1) Nearby departure airports — VPS, PNS, ECP, MOB, and MSY can all differ by $50–$150 on the same date. Check all 5 before committing. (2) Hidden city ticketing — booking a connecting flight through your destination and skipping the last leg. Only works with carry-on only and you cannot check bags. (3) One-way on two separate airlines is almost always cheaper than round-trip on one airline when one carrier has a sale on only one direction. (4) Positioning flights — drive to a bigger hub (MSY, ATL) and catch a nonstop. The gas + parking can still beat the connecting fare from VPS.
🎫 Fare Class Reality: Airlines sell the same seat at 8–12 different price levels called fare buckets. The cheapest bucket (usually 2–4 seats) sells out first. When you see a price and come back tomorrow, you may find the next bucket which is $30–$80 more. This is why you set a target and track — the low bucket appears again when cancellations happen or the airline runs a promotion. Basic economy is the cheapest bucket but has the most restrictions. Main cabin is the next bucket. Always check both before buying.

📋How To Use▼
1

Pro Tip: Allegiant VPS↔MDW drops to $59–$70 during flash sales — usually Tuesday mornings. Set target at $75.
⚠️ How Airlines Track You: Airlines raise prices using repeated searches, cookies, browser data, device type, IP location, and demand signals. To avoid triggering price inflation: (1) Always search in incognito/private window. (2) Clear cookies between searches. (3) Never search the same route more than twice in one day from the same device. (4) Use a different browser or VPN to reset your IP between sessions. (5) Avoid searching from work or school networks — corporate IPs trigger business fare pricing automatically.
📉 Price Drop Watch Rules: (1) Set target 15-20% below current — not 50%, you will miss real deals. (2) Check Tuesday and Wednesday mornings — airlines drop sale inventory overnight Monday. (3) The 21-day rule is real: lowest fares appear 3 weeks before departure on most carriers. (4) Never book Friday or Sunday — weekend demand signals inflate prices. (5) If a price drops then bounces back, it will likely drop again. Patience beats panic buying. (6) For domestic flights, 6-8 weeks out is the sweet spot — far enough for deals, close enough for inventory release.

Search in incognito always — airlines track repeated searches and inflate prices. Private window = clean slate every time.
