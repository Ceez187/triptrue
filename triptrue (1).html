Pro Tip: Southwest includes 2 free checked bags. Allegiant/Spirit/Frontier bags often cost more than the base fare.
🗺️ Hidden Route Strategies: Beyond splitting airlines, consider: (1) Nearby departure airports — VPS, PNS, ECP, MOB, and MSY can all differ by $50–$150 on the same date. Check all 5 before committing. (2) Hidden city ticketing — booking a connecting flight through your destination and skipping the last leg. Only works with carry-on only and you cannot check bags. (3) One-way on two separate airlines is almost always cheaper than round-trip on one airline when one carrier has a sale on only one direction. (4) Positioning flights — drive to a bigger hub (MSY, ATL) and catch a nonstop. The gas + parking can still beat the connecting fare from VPS.
🎫 Fare Class Reality: Airlines sell the same seat at 8–12 different price levels called fare buckets. The cheapest bucket (usually 2–4 seats) sells out first. When you see a price and come back tomorrow, you may find the next bucket which is $30–$80 more. This is why you set a target and track — the low bucket appears again when cancellations happen or the airline runs a promotion. Basic economy is the cheapest bucket but has the most restrictions. Main cabin is the next bucket. Always check both before buying.
🗓️ 1. Cheapest Booking Window
Tuesday and Wednesday departures are 15–25% cheaper than Friday and Sunday. Book on Tuesday at 1 AM EST — airlines drop fares Monday night and competitors match by Tuesday morning. For domestic flights, 3–6 weeks out is the sweet spot. International: 2–6 months.
📅 2. Flexible Dates Hack
On Google Flights, click the calendar grid view to see every date combo color-coded by price. Shifting your trip by 1–2 days can save $50–$200. The cheapest combo is almost always: depart Tuesday or Wednesday, return Monday or Tuesday. Avoid Friday outbound and Sunday return — that combo is priced for business travelers who expense it.
✈️ 3. Nearby Airport Savings
Always check airports within 1–2 hours of both your origin and destination. From NW Florida: VPS, PNS, ECP, and MOB can differ by $80–$150 on the same date. At the destination: Chicago has ORD and MDW, New York has JFK, LGA, and EWR, Dallas has DFW and DAL. The savings almost always exceed gas and parking costs for the alternate airport.
🏙️ 4. Hidden City Loophole
Sometimes a connecting flight through your destination is cheaper than flying there directly. Book the through-ticket and simply exit at your destination layover. Rules: carry-on bags ONLY (checked bags go to final destination), no return ticket on the same booking, one-time use only (airlines flag repeat users). Use Skiplagged.com to find these. Airlines hate it but it is legal.
🗺️ 5. Multi-City / Open-Jaw
An open-jaw ticket flies you into one city and out of another — and often costs less than round-tripping. Example: fly into Chicago, train to Milwaukee, fly home from Milwaukee. Or fly VPS→Vegas, drive to LA, fly LAX→VPS. Book each leg as one-way on separate airlines. Use Google Flights multi-city search to find these combinations.
⚡ 6. Mistake Fare Sniper
Airlines accidentally publish fares $200–$800 below normal. They last 2–24 hours. Track them at: Secret Flying (secretflying.com), The Flight Deal (theflightdeal.com), Scott's Cheap Flights (now Going.com), Airfarewatchdog. Set email alerts for your home airport. When a mistake fare drops — book immediately, cancel your plans later. Most airlines honor them.
💱 7. Currency / Region Pricing Exploit
The same flight can cost less on an airline's website when priced in a different country's currency. Use a VPN set to the destination country, then book on that country's version of the airline site. Works best on international routes. Also: some airlines price differently by region — Turkish Airlines, Avianca, and Qatar Airways are known for significant regional price gaps. Always check before buying at face value.

🔍 Always search incognito — airlines track repeat searches and inflate prices. New incognito window every search.
📅 Tuesday 7–10 AM EST — airlines drop fares Monday night, competitors match Tuesday morning. Best window to find drops.
⏱️ 21-day rule — lowest domestic fares appear 3 weeks before departure as unsold seats get discounted.
🚫 Never Friday or Sunday — business travel demand inflates fares 15–25% on these days.
🎯 6–8 weeks out — sweet spot for domestic booking. International: 2–6 months out.
✈️ Check all nearby airports — VPS, PNS, ECP, and MOB can differ $50–$150 on the same date. Always compare all 4.
